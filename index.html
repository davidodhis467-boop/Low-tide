Anchor points

Things to hold onto when the current gets strong

Not motivational posters. Just the things that are actually true, from people who almost walked away and didn't.

On rest

Rest is not quitting

Stepping away from the keyboard is not the same as stepping away from the craft. Your best debugging happens in the shower, not the sprint.

On comparison

Compare today-you to yesterday-you

Not to the senior engineer three years ahead. Not to the GitHub streak of a stranger. Just to who you were the last time you opened this editor.

On confusion

Confused means learning, not failing

The feeling of "I have no idea what I'm doing" is the exact texture of a brain building new structure. It's supposed to feel like this.

On asking

Asking for help is a senior move

Every engineer you admire has a Slack history full of "dumb questions." The junior move is suffering in silence to protect your ego.

On pace

Slow is a pace, not a verdict

You're allowed to take the scenic route through this field. Nobody's handing out medals for who arrived first — only for who stayed.

On identity

You are not your last error message

A failing build describes your code for a moment. It says nothing at all about whether you belong here.

"Every engineer still in this industry has a folder somewhere called 'final_final_v2' and a night they almost deleted it all."
— every senior dev, eventually, over coffee
